Section 4.1 - Governing Board

The institution has a governing board of at least five members that:

a) is the legal body with specific authority over the institution.

b) exercises fiduciary oversight of the institution.

c) ensures that both the presiding officer of the board and a majority of other voting members of the board are free of any contractual, employment, personal, or familial financial interest in the institution.

d) is not controlled by a minority of board members or by organizations or institutions separate from it.

e) is not presided over by the chief executive officer of the institution [CR]

Judgments

Narrative:

Florida Gulf Coast University (FGCU) governing board ensures both the presence of viable leadership and strong financial resources to fulfill the institutional mission. FGCU’s governing board is absent of undue influence from external sources.

Legal body with specific authority over the institution

FGCU operates under a Board of Trustees (BOT) consisting of 13 members, as mandated by the State Constitution IX, Section 7. This Board collectively holds lawful authority over the university. The BOT convenes at least four times annually, specifically in January, April, June, and September. Additional meetings may be scheduled if necessary to address pertinent matters. During these meetings, the Board addresses various items on its agenda including reports from representatives of shared governance at FGCU, such as the Faculty Senate President, the President of the Staff Advisory Council, Student Government, and the FGCU President (the university's administration). Additionally, the agenda encompasses informational items and action items falling under the purview of the Board's standing committees.

The legal authority bestowed upon the BOT is outlined in the Board of Governors (BOG) Regulation 1.001. As the active policy-making body for the university, the Board's approved rules are implemented through the promulgation of University Regulations. Over the past four years (Fall 2019-Fall 2023), the FGCU Board of Trustees has thoroughly reviewed and approved eight new regulations, repealed five, and approved 62 amendments/revisions to existing regulations.

Exercises fiduciary oversight of the institution.

The FGCU Board of Trustees holds the responsibility of ensuring the availability of sufficient financial resources to support a robust educational program at the institution. According to the BOG Regulation 1.001, the Board of Trustees is tasked with the "financial management of its university." This involves submitting a comprehensive institutional budget request, which includes both fixed capital outlay and operating budget components, to the Board of Governors for approval in accordance with established guidelines.

The Board of Trustees is required to prepare an Accountability Plan for the Board of Governors. This document outlines the University's key priorities, strategic directions, specific actions, and financial plans aimed at achieving those priorities. It also includes performance expectations and outcomes related to institutional and system-wide goals, all within the framework of the University's unique mission and core strengths, while considering the Strategic Plan goals (11) of the State University System and the needs of the region and state.

On an annual basis, the Board of Trustees conducts a thorough review and approval of the University's budget. This budget is designed to ensure adequate resources are allocated to support a sound educational program. The Board receives regular reports on the financial performance of the University during its quarterly meetings, presented by the university administration. Furthermore, the results of annual state financial audits are also presented to the Board, providing additional insight into the financial health and accountability of the institution.

Free of any contractual, employment, personal, or familial financial interest in the institution

According to the Board of Governors Regulation 1.001 (2(a)) the University Board of Trustees consists of six members appointed by the Governor of the State of Florida and five members appointed by the Board of Governors. All appointees must undergo Senate confirmation. Among the Board members, two are employed by the University itself, the Faculty Senate President serves as a full-time employee, while the Student Government President receives a salary as a part-time employee.

It is important to note that the Chair of the Board, as well as the other members, maintain no contractual, employment, personal, or familial interest in the institution. The Board of Trustees adheres to a stringent Ethnics Policy, clearly outlining the standards that Board members must follow to ensure that their fiduciary responsibilities are carried out without any self-interest. Throughout the University's history, there have been no accusations of Board members violating their duties or breaching this policy.

As a testament to their dedication and understanding of their role, Board of Trustee Members review and sign Conflict of Interest Statements. This action underscores their commitment to transparency and upholding their responsibilities with the utmost integrity.

Not controlled by a minority of board members or by organizations or institutions separate from it

The FGCU Board of Trustees operates independently, free from control by any minority of board members or external organizations or interests. The Board has the flexibility to function as a Committee of the Whole, and it maintains standing Committees of the Whole for Audit and Compliance; Finance, Facilities and Administration; Academic/Student/Faculty Affairs and Officers Nominating. Decisions made by the Board are determined through a majority vote of its members, ensuring a collective decision-making process.

The University Board of Trustees is bound by the State's Public Business and Public Records Laws (Sunshine Laws), which dictate the manner in which the Board conducts its operations. These laws ensure transparency and accountability by requiring the Board to adhere to open meetings and public records provisions, fostering a culture of openness and accessibility in its proceedings.

He was awarded Hardware Industry’s Top Gun Award presented by the National Retail Hardware Association in 2014, Finalist for the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year Award in 2014, Junior Achievement’s Business Hall of Fame Laureate in 2014, 2017 FGCU Alumni of the Year, voted the 2017 Gulfshore Business Best Boss for a Large Company, Business Observer’s 2019 Icons of Business and awarded the 2019 Naples Daily News Outstanding Citizen of the Year.

Not presided over by the chief executive officer of the institution.

At Florida Gulf Coast University, there is a distinct division of roles between the chief executive officer and the presiding officer of the Board. The institution has a dedicated chief executive officer, Dr. Aysegul Timur, whose primary responsibility lies with the University itself. As outlined in the employment agreement, Dr. Timur serves as the President, carrying out specific duties and responsibilities. Notably, Dr. Timur also holds the position of Secretary of the Corporation, further reinforcing their focus on institutional matters.

The role of presiding officer, which entails leading the FGCU Board of Trustees, is held by Mr. Michael Wynn, the Chair of the Board. This separation of roles ensures that the President can solely concentrate on their obligations to the university, while the presiding officer oversees the operations of the Board, providing effective leadership in that capacity.
