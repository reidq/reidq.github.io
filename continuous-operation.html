Section 3.1c - Continuous Operation

An institution seeking to gain or maintain accredited status has degree-granting authority from the appropriate government agency or agencies. [CR]

Judgments

Narrative:

Florida Gulf Coast University was established by the Florida Legislature in 1991 and has been continuously in operation, with students enrolled in degree programs. In Fall 2023, the institution had a total enrollment of 16,212 students (13,815 undergraduate, 1,891 Graduate, and 506 non-degree students) The university has five academic colleges and two schools (website). These colleges and schools offer programs of study leading to a baccalaureate degree in 57 program areas, a master's degree in 24 different program areas, and a terminal in 3 degree program areas. Enrollment summaries are published quarterly and listed in Institutional Research website where a history of annual enrollment shows continuous operation since last comprehensive review in 2015.
