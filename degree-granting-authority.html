Section 3.1a - Degree-granting authority

An institution seeking to gain or maintain accredited status has degree-granting authority from the appropriate government agency or agencies. [CR]

Judgments

Narrative:

Florida Gulf Coast University (FGCU) was approved by the Legislature in 1991 as the 10th institution in the State University System of Florida serving Southwest Florida. The State University System (SUS) was established in the State's constitution and is defined in Florida Statutes. FGCU has duly received degree-granting authority (page 1) from the Florida Board of Governors, the designated government agency of the State of Florida (as stated in the Florida Constitution) that governs the 12-institution State University System of Florida.

The State's Constitution grants broad powers to the Board of Governors to be "fully responsible for the management of the whole university system." In defining its management responsibilities, the Board of Governors adopted BOG Regulation 1.001 that delegates each of the SUS institutional boards’ authorization of new degree programs. At FGCU, this is found in FGCU Policy 2.006.

Changes to Academic Degree Program Offerings

Annually, in accordance with Florida Board of Governors’ Regulation 2.002, Florida Gulf Coast University (FGCU) submits an accountability plan (2023) to the Florida Board of Governors. As part of this plan, FGCU includes a list of new academic degree program proposals that the university intends to submit to its board of trustees over the next three years. A list of new programs and program closures since the last comprehensive review in 2015 included in Part 2 of this document and Standard 14.2 Substantive Changes.

Off-Campus Sites

The Florida Board of Governors delegates general degree-granting authority to Florida Gulf Coast University, which also applies to offering degree programs at off-campus instructional sites. FGCU opened the two off-campus instructional sites since the last comprehensive review in 2015.

Distance Learning

In 2017, Florida Gulf Coast University (FGCU) joined the State Authorization Reciprocity Agreement (SARA) and received approval from both the National Council for State Authorization Reciprocity Agreements (NC-SARA) and the Florida State Authorization Reciprocity Agreement (FL-SARA). This membership allows FGCU to enroll students in online degree programs from all 50 states and most territories, including Puerto Rico, Guam, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. Information about these agreements is available on the FGCU website (NC SARA, State Authorization). Additionally, FGCU’s status as a participating SARA institution in Florida can be found on the NC-SARA and FL- SARA websites.
